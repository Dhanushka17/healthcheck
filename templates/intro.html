Introduction to BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple and widely used measure to estimate a person's body fat based on weight and height. It is calculated using the formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)2. It helps classify individuals into categories such as Underweight: BMI<18.5 Normal weight:18.5-24.9 Overweight:25-29.9 Obesity:BMI>30 Why BMI is Important? BMI is important because it provides a quick and reliable way to assess weight relative to height and estimate body fat percentage. It's used as a screening tool for identigying individuals at increased risk of chronic diseases abd can help guide healthcare providers in developing personalised health plans.
